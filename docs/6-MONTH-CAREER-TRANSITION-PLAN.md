## Adaptation Strategies

### If You're Falling Behind:
1. **Reduce scope:** Focus on most common patterns (arrays, strings, trees, graphs)
2. **Quality over quantity:** Deeply understand 100 problems vs superficially solving 200
3. **Use solutions:** Don't spend more than 30 min stuck—learn from solutions
4. **Extend timeline:** Better to be well-prepared in 7-8 months than rushed in 6

### If You're Ahead:
1. **Dive deeper:** Study advanced topics (tries, segment trees, advanced DP)
2. **Contribute to open source:** Build credibility
3. **Write technical blogs:** Demonstrate expertise
4. **Start applying earlier:** Month 4 instead of Month 5

### Staying Motivated:
1. **Track progress:** Use a spreadsheet to log problems solved
2. **Join communities:** LeetCode discussions, Discord groups
3. **Study buddies:** Find peers for mock interviews
4. **Celebrate small wins:** Every problem solved is progress
5. **Remember your 'why':** Software architect role is your goal

## Risk Mitigation

### What Could Go Wrong:

1. **Burnout Risk:**
   - **Mitigation:** Build in rest days, don't overstudy weekends
   - One day per week is "light study" or rest

2. **Interview Failures:**
   - **Mitigation:** Start applying early, treat first interviews as practice
   - Need 20-30 applications to get 2-3 offers

3. **Knowledge Gaps During Interviews:**
   - **Mitigation:** Be honest, show learning ability
   - "I haven't worked with X, but here's how I'd approach learning it"

4. **Age Bias (38 years old):**
   - **Mitigation:** Emphasize leadership, maturity, and depth of experience
   - Target roles valuing senior experience

5. **Time Management:**
   - **Mitigation:** Block calendar, wake up 1 hour earlier if needed
   - Treat study time as non-negotiable meetings
